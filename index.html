Asia is the largest and most diverse continent in the world, offering a rich tapestry of cultures, languages, landscapes, and traditions. From the futuristic skylines of Japan and South Korea to the ancient temples of Cambodia and India, Asia blends the past and present in fascinating ways. Countries like Thailand and Indonesia are famous for their natural beauty and vibrant tourism, while China and India are global economic powerhouses with deep historical roots. The Middle East showcases a mix of Islamic heritage and modern development, particularly in places like the UAE and Saudi Arabia. Each country in Asia has its own unique charm, making the continent a captivating destination for travelers and a vital region in global affairs.

The term engineering is derived from the word engineer, which itself dates back to the 14th century when an engine'er (literally, one who builds or operates a siege engine) referred to "a constructor of military engines".[7] In this context, now obsolete, an "engine" referred to a military machine, i.e., a mechanical contraption used in war (for example, a catapult). Notable examples of the obsolete usage which have survived to the present day are military engineering corps, e.g., the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers.
